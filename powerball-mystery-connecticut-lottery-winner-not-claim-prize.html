Powerball mystery grips Connecticut town as $185m winner fails to come forward amid rush to find the

AN $185million Powerball lottery winner in Connecticut has not yet come forward to claim their prize, sparking a furious search to find them before their ticket expires.

The winner bought their ticket from a One Stop store in Cheshire ahead of the Valentines' Day draw.

They picked the five correct numbers as well as the Powerball number.

The winning numbers were: 16-25-27-49-55, the Powerball was 17 and the multiplier was 3.

And, the player has until August 13 to claim their prize, according to Connecticut Lottery.

It’s reported that the winner can expect to receive more than $123million when deductions have been applied.

They can also opt to get their winnings split into payments totaling $20million each.

The odds of scooping the prize are one in 292,201,338 and it’s the ninth Powerball jackpot to be won in the state.

Connecticut Lottery officials revealed on their website that the prize remains unclaimed, sparking excitement in the area.

Mike Sadiq is the owner of the convenience store that sold the winning ticket, Fox61 reported.

He was presented with a bonus check worth $100,000 for selling the ticket.

Sadiq has called his regular shoppers but no one has so far come forward to claim the prize.

The store owner claims that the ticket was the biggest amount he had ever sold.

He told Fox: “I had twice a half a million-dollar winner and two or three times $100,000.”

And, local resident Denise Killis said she can’t wait to find out who the winner is.

The big win came as the Connecticut Lottery marked its 50th anniversary.

It comes as a lucky woman from Maryland used the license plates of her wrecked car as inspiration before winning $25,000.

She bought a 50-cent ticket, using the numbers from the license plate of her vehicle that had recently been towed away after it was involved in a minor car crash.
