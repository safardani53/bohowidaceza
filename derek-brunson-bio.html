Derek Brunson – College Wrestling

Derek has won regional championships multiple times in his career. In fact, he was honored as an NCAA-All-American three times from the University of North Carolina.

Moreover, he had a total of 121 victories throughout his college and school career.

Derek Brunson – Professional Career

Soon after graduating, Derek turned his focus from wrestling to Mixed Martial Arts. After a while, he made his professional debut in May 2010 against John Bryant.

He is nicknamed ‘The Human Blanket’.

Furthermore, Brunson won the bout via a rear-naked choke in the 52nd second of the first round. Thereafter, Derek went on an unbeaten streak of six wins and zero losses before getting picked up by Strikeforce.

Subsequently, the 36-year-old fought five times, winning three and losing the rest. Then, in 2012, Brunson signed with the UFC to compete in The Ultimate Fighter 17. However, Derek had to withdraw from the tournament due to an injury.

Eventually, he made his UFC debut against Chris Leben at UFC 155. Moreover, Brunson’s opportunity came in a stroke of luck as he was called in last minute to replace Karlos Vemola.

Despite being a heavy favorite to lose, the North Carolina native utilized his collegiate wrestling background to dominate his opponent. As a consequence, Derek won the bout via a unanimous decision.

Since then, Brunson has fought 16 times in the UFC, winning 11 and losing five in the process. Although Derek has won 11 fights, he has lost all of his match-ups against top middleweight contenders or former champions.

As a result, the 36-year-old has not fought for the UFC Middleweight Championship yet. Having said that, Brunson is on a high after defeating Elias Theodorou and Ian Heinrich in his last two fights.

Derek Brunson: UFC 248

After his impressive victory at UFC 241, Brunson was booked to face Edmen Shahbazyan at UFC 248. But, the fight got canceled due to unspecified reasons.
