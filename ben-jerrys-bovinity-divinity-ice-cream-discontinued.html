BEN & Jerry’s has discontinued a beloved ice cream and laid the treat to rest in the brand’s flavor graveyard.

Fans have urged the ice cream giant to bring back its Bovinity Divinity dessert.

Bovinity Divinity was a huge hit among dessert fans between 1998 and 2001 before it was consigned to the flavor graveyard.

The ice cream makers added dark and white fudge cows to a mixture of milk chocolate and white ice creams.

Bovinity Divinity has been discontinued for more than 20 years but nostalgic Ben & Jerry’s fans have called for the frozen treat to make a comeback.

Mickey (@MickeyGuitar95) tweeted: “Please do a guy a favor and bring back Bovinity Divinity for a limited time.

"This flavor kicked the bucket when angels took the herd."

Bovinity Divinity is joined by more than two dozen flavors in the brand’s ice cream graveyard.

Ben & Jerry’s has also axed its Dublin Mudslide, Crème Brulee, Cool Britannia, and Chocolate Macadamia flavors.

Ice cream lovers have described the flavor of its Crème Brulee ice cream as “creamy and sweet.”

The dessert first hit the shelves in 2007 before being axed in 2012.

A fan wrote in a product review on OnSecondScoop.com: “You’re able to detect some notes of caramel flavor but the thing I liked most about the swirl was that it adds a unique texture to the pint.

"It breaks easily on your spoon but has a crisp crunch when you bite into it."

The flavor has been compared to the custard used in the popular dessert.

Ben & Jerry’s has also discontinued iconic flavors including Schweddy Balls, Wavy Gravy, and Fossil Fuel.

Fossil Fuel was made up of ice cream that contained chocolate cookie pieces, fudge dinosaur shapes, and a fudge swirl.
