Sue Barker's Wimbledon absence labelled a huge 'bummer' as John McEnroe likens BBC host to legendary

SUE BARKER’s absence from Wimbledon TV screens next week is going to be a major “bummer” – just like when Bjorn Borg suddenly quit tennis in 1982.

Former French Open champion Barker will be watching most of the action from her own sofa while occasionally attending the odd day in SW19 hospitality.

This is the first time in three decades that Barker, 67, has not fronted the coverage for the BBC – with safe-pair-of-hands Clare Balding taking the reins from Monday onwards.

So many times on air, Barker had to play the school headmistress role, ensuring that maverick McEnroe was kept on the straight and narrow while chatting on commentary duty.

For McEnroe, the sadness he will experience this time is akin to how he felt when his great rival Borg, a five-time Wimbledon winner, threw away his racquets more than 40 years ago.

The American said: “No Sue? That’s going to be a bummer.
