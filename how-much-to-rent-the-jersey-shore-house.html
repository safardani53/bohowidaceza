The 9-person cast made the Jersey house home for four out of their six seasons and as it turns out, you can rent the Jersey Shore house too, if you really wanted. Here how much it goes for.

What does the 'Jersey Shore' house look like?

You, too, can fist-bump your way through summer and make lasting memories, maybe not as rowdy as the Jersey Shore cast. The actual home that Jersey Shore was filmed in is available to rent.

The home, which is a six-bedroom, one-bathroom vacation house, is located just moments away from Seaside Heights and the famous beach. The home itself is just over 1600-square-feet and can accommodate up to 11 guests which bodes well for a summer full of fun.

The rental allows for full access of the house, including the outdoor dining area, the balcony and terrace, and for super fans of the show, the iconic duck phone is still plugged in to the wall and works.

"MTV's Jersey Shore House, which is located in Seaside Heights, just 200 m away from Casino Pier and a few steps away from Jersey Shore Beach House," the listing reads on Booking.com. "This holiday home features air-conditioned accommodations with a balcony."

Scrolling through the pictures, the home looks to be just the same in terms of decorations as when Jersey Shore is filming. Complete with the sunset backdrop where the cast members give their interviews in the show.

How much to rent the 'Jersey Shore' house?

The cost of renting the Jersey Shore house varies based on what season of the year the vacation home is being booked for, and how long the stay is going to be. The summer is likely the most busy season, which is the case for most vacation homes. 

Article continues below advertisement

Generally speaking, the home will cost up to $3000 per night, but if you wanted to stay for a full week in the middle of summer, it could cost as high as $25,000 total on Booking.com. 

Looking at Seaside Reality NJ, they list the cost of renting at $2000 per night, however, the site does list that multiple nights rates and party packages are available. 

Article continues below advertisement

While the stay isn't going to be the cheapest vacation ever, for anyone who is a fan of Jersey Shore, it would be hard to top a stay at the actual house where all the shenanigans took place. 

Chances are there won't be any further seasons of Jersey Shore filmed at the home in Seaside Heights. According to US Weekly, the show reportedly had some issues filming in the area during the first season of Jersey Shore: Family Vacation, which influenced their decision to move locations for Season 2.
