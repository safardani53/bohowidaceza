James Robert Cade is an American physician, university professor, researcher and inventor. Cade, originally from Texas, graduated in medicine from the University of Texas, and became professor of medicine and nephrology at the University of Florida. Although Cade is involved in many areas of medical research, he is best remembered as the leader of the team that created the Gatorade sports drink. Gatorade will have important medical applications for treating dehydration in patients and has generated more than $ 150 million in royalties for the university. In his later years, Cade became a prominent philanthropist, donating substantial sums to charities affiliated with the Lutheran Church, creating scholarships and free donations to the University of Florida and others. colleges and universities, and also donating their own charitable funds.
