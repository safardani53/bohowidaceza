Jeopardy! fans 'frustrated' as game show makes it 'unclear' if new episode is airing on Thanksgiving

JEOPARDY! fans have been left confused over whether the game show will air on Thanksgiving.

Viewers felt producers could be clearer on when the next episode will be shown and flooded social media with their complaints.

One frustrated fan asked on a Jeopardy! Facebook group: "Is Jeopardy! on Thanksgiving?"

Another replied: "Depends on your station. The show is on, but individual station programming may displace or preempt it."

Others felt sure that a new episode will be aired on Thursday, commenting: "It usually is - has been past few years."

Another agreed: "Just like every year since the Jeopardy revival started with its revival in 1984. Jeopardy airs on Thanksgiving."

The show will indeed be airing on Thursday evening.

The backlash comes after viewers felt confused about the number of games in the Tournament Of Champions final.

Host Ken Jennings, 48, said at the start of the first finals: "This year, this will be no mere two-day total point affair [or a two-episode finish]."

"Instead, it will be a best-of-seven series - in which the first player to notch three victories will be declared our winner," he announced.

Fans were excited that finals would be longer, but were left in shambles over the term "best of seven."

In fact, the winner will be the first player to win three matches.

Jeopardy! legend Amy Schneider ended up winning the Tournament of Champions crown on Monday, November 21.

Amy, 43, has faced 5-time champ Andrew He and fan-favorite professor's tournament winner Sam Buttrey in six ferocious finals games.

On Monday - with two wins for Amy, two for Andrew, and one for Sam - the TV star with the second-longest Jeopardy! streak ever (with $1.3million) sealed the deal.

Going into Final Jeopardy, it could have been anyone's game.

Amy had $15,600, Andrew right behind at $14,200 and $8,000.

Under the category "Plays" it read: "The January 12th, 1864 Washington Evening Star reported on this 'dashing comedy' to a 'full and delighted house.'"

The correct answer was the infamous "Our Country Cousin," and Amy ensured her bet would not be upstaged.

She wagered a huge $13,000 as the crowd gasped - blocking out Andrew's maximum by $200 if correct - she won with $28,600.
