Curtis Samuel | Professional career

Curtis’s professional football career started once the Carolina Panthers selected him as the 40th overall pick at the 2017 NFL Draft.

Likewise, he made his NFL debut on September 17, 2017, against the Buffalo Bills and proudly made his first NFL victory, recording his first two career receptions for seven yards.

Sadly, just during the beginning of his rookie season Curtis sustained a left ankle injury while playing against Miami Dolphins in the middle of the 2017 season and got placed on an injured reserve, which abruptly ended his rookie season. 

Moreover, Samuel also missed the first three games of 2018 due to his injury.

After a long recovery, he returned to action on October 7, 2018, and recorded his first touchdown against the New York Giants.

Further, Curtis played against several teams between the 2018-2020 seasons and recorded victories against the Arizona Cardinals and Detroit Lions while playing.
