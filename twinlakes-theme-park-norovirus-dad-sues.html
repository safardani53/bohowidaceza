Dad sues Twinlakes theme park as he and son, 3, among dozens of visitors to fall ill after norovirus

A DAD is suing a theme park after his three-year-old son and dozens of other children were left ill after a Norovirus outbreak.

Twinlakes Family Theme Park in Melton Mowbray, Leics., was forced to close its water park after visitors suffered sickness and diarrhoea.

A probe was launched by Public Health England and confirmed those who fell ill at the water park had contracted "vomiting bug" Norovirus.

Natan Khareghat, 28, has now launched legal action against the park after he and young son Leo fell ill following a trip to the Au Guang Dragon Zone Water Park area on July 19.

The housing association officer, from Nottingham, said he was forced to take two days off work sick after the visit - with lawyers from Irwin Mitchell assisting him with his claim.

He added: "The past week has been awful. It was bad enough that I was sick, but to see Leo so unwell was heartbreaking.

"Since our visit to Twinlakes, I have heard there are many other people complaining of similar symptoms.

It is frustrating that I had to take time off work due to something which was not my fault, and I hope that appropriate measures are taken so this doesn't happen again

Natan Khareghat

"To now be told that there is Norovirus present is very distressing, but it would explain why we were both feeling so ill.

"It is frustrating that I had to take time off work due to something which was not my fault, and I hope that appropriate measures are taken so this doesn't happen again."

An end-of-year trip to the theme park also left "at least 30 kids suffering throughout the following night", according to reports.

The schoolkids from Springbank Primary School, Eastwood, headed to Twinlakes Family Theme Park on Wednesday for an end of year treat.

But on Thursday morning, Facebook was flooded with worried parents describing their children's symptoms of nausea and diarrhoea.

One mum told the BBC she was worried even before her kids entered the "absolutely disgusting" water.

The 'vomiting bug' - What is Norovirus?

Norovirus is one of the most common stomach bugs in the UK and is also referred to as “the winter vomiting bug”, although it can affect people all year round.

It is very unpleasant but it usually clears itself up in a few days.

The virus spreads easily spreads around public places and is transmitted when a tiny particle of vomit or poo from an infected person gets into someone else’s mouth.

You can also catch it if you are in close proximity to an infected person and they breathe on you.

According to the NHS website, you are likely to have caught Norovirus if you experience a sudden sick feeling, projectile vomiting and watery diarrhoea.

Other symptoms can include a slight fever, headaches, painful cramps and aching limbs.

A Public Health England spokesperson said: "If you are visiting a swimming pool over the summer season, people with diarrhoea or vomiting should not use swimming pools whilst symptomatic, or for 48 hours after symptoms have completely ceased.

"It is very important to follow the 48 hour exclusion advice, even if if you are feeling better after a short period of time.

"This incident illustrates how quickly infections can spread in environments like swimming pools and water parks.

"Personal hygiene, with thorough handwashing with soap and water before eating and after going to the toilet is very important in reducing the spread of infection."
