Watch as barn find explorer reveals Range Rover gold mine - including 4x4 used by Ed Sheeran in Cast

A BARN find expert has discovered a Range Rover gold mine - including one 4x4 used by Ed Sheeran in his Castle on the Hill music video.

The owner of the collection, Rob Marsden, has 50 classic Range Rovers, and contacted the specialist explorer who travelled to Dover to see what could be one of the UK's best barn finds.

YouTuber @imstozke took to social media to show his subscribers how the cars are holding up after being stored in a barn for several years.

He said: "This is the first time for me. I don't usually get permission to look around an exclusive collection like this one.

"The chap who owns this collection has collected them for many years, rescued these cars from field barns, and bought them at auction.

"He's now got to the point where it's completely taking over his unit, so he's now downsizing, so he wants all the vehicles to be sold."

The first car the poster shows in his video is an RSK Range Rover, but it needed some extensive repair work inside.

Then, he finds the special edition Range Rover featured in Ed Sheran's music video, Castle on the Hill.

This particular Range Rover was 23rd off the production line in the last 25 first-generation Range Rovers ever made in February 1996.

It's a V8 petrol automatic and is just one of 50 Mr Marsden owns at his company, Classic Range Rover.

The explorer opens the car boot and finds the album cover of Ed Sheran inside.

He reveals: "Ed Sheran was going to buy this car, but the deal fell through. He drives this car in the music video."

The car apparently is in immaculate condition and includes brown leather seats.

As he explores, he finds one Land Rover Range Rover that was manufactured in 1995, with its last MOT expiring in 2011.

Then, he comes across a red Range Rover with original paint, carpets and only 6,557 miles on the clock.

The video has had more than 177,128 views on Youtube and 273 comments.

Users were blown away by this barn find - and most say that they love the content of this YouTuber.

One said: "I would love to see more of these videos on top of the usual abandoned ones. Seeing massive collections by car enthusiasts and classic collectors alike."

Another said: "Wow, there's almost no words!! Amazing collection. Glad the owner let you in to share them."

A third pointed out: "It's a passion bordering on obsession. I would love to see all these beautiful cars on the road someday."

This comes after a barn find explorer discovered a classic Ford Capri that has covered less than 60k miles in 40 years.
