Britain's Got Talent returns with fully nude comedy stripper, Diversity Junior and death-defying ill

BRITAIN'S Got Talent returns tomorrow night with a typically bizarre and eclectic range of acts on display.

Internet sensation Mr Uekusa gives the judges and audience an eyeful as he whips a towel off his genitals while balancing china on his nether region and nipples.

Will the 30-year-old comedian from Japan be too much for a British audience?

Ashley Banjo was catapulted to stardom when his dance group Diversity won the show in 2009, now his proteges DVJ (Diversity Junior) will attempt to replicate their mentor's success.

Escapologist Matt Johnson will have viewers' hearts in their mouths when he tries another death-defying stunt.

The 43-year-old's equipment let him down last year with his glass water tank shattering, and he's decided to test himself before the judges once again this time around.

There's an abundance of variety making for one of the best opening episodes ever.

B-Positive Choir is the official choir of NHS Blood and Transplant and was set up to encourage people to donate blood.

Will the 48-strong collective receive a positive response to their version of Andra Day's Rise Up.

Simon Cowell famously isn't a big fan of magicians so 46-year-old Marc Spellman might find his mind-reading routine a hard sell.

The Sun previously reported how emotions will run high in the first episode as Ant McPartlin bursts into tears while watching an incredible performance.

The touching scenes will have viewers reaching for their box of tissues as the Geordie TV legend and his co-stars all struggled to stop crying throughout the audition.

Ant, 42, will appear on screen for the next few weeks as part of the BGT audition process.

But his co-star Declan Donnelly will be hosting the show solo when the live episodes begin later this month after Ant went back to rehab following his “drink-drive” crash.

In the first episode, which airs on Saturday night, Ant is back on top form as he appears on screen alongside Dec, Simon Cowell, Amanda Holden, David Walliams and Alesha Dixon.

But although there are plenty of laughs and jokes, Ant struggled to contain himself during one incredibly moving performance.

He wells up at the side of the stage before looking at the camera and saying: “I can’t stop the tears from falling…”

He wasn’t the only one who struggled to contain his emotion during the performance, with Amanda, Simon and Alesha also wiping away tears as they watched.

Britain’s Got Talent airs on Saturday 14th April, 8pm on ITV.
