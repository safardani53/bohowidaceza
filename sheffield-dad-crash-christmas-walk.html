Sheffield crash: Bystanders lift car in battle to save dad Christian Marriott hit and killed on Xmas

THIS is the heartbreaking moment bystanders tried to lift a car in the battle to save a dad after he was ploughed down.

Christian Marriott, 46, was hit and killed when he tried helping an unconscious stranger while on a walk with his kids, eight and six, in Sheffield, on Wednesday.

The dad-of-two was caught up in the ram attack following "violent disorder" among guest at a wedding party.

Emergency services were called to College Close, Burngreave, at around 2pm after reports of a heated row between two families.

It's understood a disagreement was sparked at the groom's house - resulting in the horrifying mow down, cops have said.

Hard-to-watch footage now shows ambulance crews and neighbours attempting to rescue Christian and other victims from under a blue Seat.

South Yorkshire Police said that Christian was a "Good Samaritan" who had stopped to help a woman knocked out on the ground.

It is believed she was involved in the altercation shortly before Christian stumbled upon her while out with his family.

Kind-hearted Christian, who was also known as Chris, was out for a post-Christmas walk with his wife and two sons.

He is then said to have left his family and stopped to provide first aid to her, before the car then collided with him, the woman, and a number of others.

Several people suffered injuries during the incident including an off-duty midwife, who had also stopped to help.

The woman Chris was helping remains in hospital in a life-threatening condition, cops added.

Another man suffered serious injuries and three women and a man have all been left with minor injuries.

Tarek Nayli, 49, who lives nearby said people were fighting in the street as panicked neighbours screamed of people trapped.

Tarek said: "I heard a lot of sound, a lot of voices, a lot of shouting.
"When I came out, I found people all gathering around and people fighting.

"One guy had a lot of blood on his face.

"The whole neighbourhood was out. They said there were people underneath the car and when the police came they managed to get them out.

"When the police came, they flipped the car over and they took two people out.

"It was very, very sad and also very upsetting and I couldn't believe it."

A murder probe has since been launched - with two men, 23 and 55, in police custody.

Both have been arrested on suspicion of murder and attempted murder.

Tributes have poured in for Chris, who worked for a charity and was a member of the local church.

His church group has said they were "deeply shocked" by his death.

A statement read: "Chris died doing what so many will remember him for, helping others.

"He had stopped to help an unconscious woman lying in the street and as he was providing first aid, a car collided with Chris, the woman and others.

"Our thoughts and prayers are obviously with his wife, and their two young sons.

"This is an unbelievably awful situation and we are heartbroken for the family.

"Please pray for them, for Chris' wider family and friends, and for City Church Sheffield as they try to process this loss."

Senior Investigating Officer, DCI Andrew Knowles, said: "This is an utterly heartbreaking case in which a Good Samaritan, who had stepped in to help a stranger in their time of need, has lost his life.

"Chris leaves behind a loving family including his devastated wife and two young sons.

"We are absolutely determined to secure justice for Chris and his loved ones following this horrific tragedy.

"Increased patrols will continue in the area in the coming days and local people will continue to see officers carrying out enquiries, including going house-to-house to gather witness statements.

"There are people in that community who hold information which is vital to our investigation and have not yet given statements.

"I want to be able to get to the bottom of what happened for Chris and his family.
