Career

  • Jansrud finished eighth in the combined at the 2006 Winter Olympics in Turin. In the Olympic giant slalom, he injured his thumb, thereby ending his 2006 season. He missed the whole 2007 season due to a bulging disc detected in September. Jansrud won his first World Cup podium in Adelboden in January 2009 and placed ninth in the super combined at the World Championships in February.
  • Jansrud won his first World Cup race on home snow in Kvitfjell in March 2012, and he finished on the podium in all three-speed events throughout the weekend, culminating with a victory in the super-G on Sunday.
  • Jansrud won gold in the downhill at the 2019 World Championships in Sweden by two-hundredths of a second, beating out Svindal in his last international race.
