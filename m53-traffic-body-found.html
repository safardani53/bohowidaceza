M53 traffic brought to a standstill after BODY is found on central reservation

A BODY has been found on the central reservation of the M53 closing the motorway to rush hour traffic in Merseyside.

Police closed the motorway yesterday evening, after motorists reported seeing a body, and have warned it could remain shut in both directions between Junctions 2 and 3 into this evening's rush hour.

The man's body was found during a search of the motorway by police and it is believed he died in a collision.

No-one else has been reported injured and police are appealing for any witnesses.

A number of people have already come forward to help officers with their enquiries.

Police are working to establish the identity of the man and trace his next of kin.

A spokesman for Merseyside Police said: "We would like to thank everyone affected by the motorway closure for their patience and understanding while police officers and the other agencies involved deal with this serious incident."
