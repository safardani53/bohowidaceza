Education

Regarding his educational background, Spencer Brown completed his early education at Lenox High School, located in Lenox, Iowa. After his high school graduation, he then enrolled at the University of Nothern Iowa, where he majored in business.

Spencer Brown | Career

Before football, Spencer Brown played basketball and golf and ran track at Lenox High School. Coming from a small hometown, their games had an eight-player team where he played as both tight end and defensive end.

Back then, Spencer did not expect college football to be in his future and thought, coming from a small-town background, he would be away from the field after high school.

“Never thought I would never even put it in my head. I just thought, ‘OK, I’m a small-town kid, we play high school football for four years, and then we go on with our lives.'”
– Spencer Brown

After his high school ended, Spencer joined the University of Northern Iowa, where he played his first season as a redshirt. During his tenure at UNI, Spencer was loyal to his team, and even after he had offers from Power 5 schools, he declined them.

Beer Fine

In December 2021, the NFL fined $5k Spencer Brown for his touchdown celebration in the team’s win over the Carolina Panthers. After Steffan Diggs had passed a touchdown, Brown took a fan’s Bud Light and proceeded to crush the can and drink its remnants on the field.

Indeed, everyone cheered at the moment and created a huge buzz; however, the NFL notified Brown of a fine later. Right after, Spencer tweeted a tweet joking about it being the most expensive Bud Light that he’s ever had in his life.

Later, he met up with that fan and dropped off cases of Bud Light to the fan. Following this, the fans learned about Brown’s charity of choice, Mission 22, and started donating to the cause.

For those unknown, this charity is an Oregon-based organization that offers programs for veterans and active duty members of the military and their families. The group also helps raise awareness of these issues and funds memorials.
