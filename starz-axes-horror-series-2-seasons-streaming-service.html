THE lights have stopped shinning for the Starz series Shining Vale.

The show, which stars Courtney Cox, has been cancelled after only two seasons.

The former Friends star played a woman who begins seeing and communicating with ghosts.

Mira Sorvino, Greg Kinnear and Judith Light appeared as well.

Starz reportedly axed the show due to low viewership. The pay network is taking the horror-comedy off their streaming platform as well at the end of the year.

Co-creator Jeff Astrof revealed the news on X, the platform formerly known as Twitter.

Sharing a photo of the cast, he tweeted: "This show gave me unrelenting joy to work on. Not a single bad day.

"Please watch the #starz (turns out, Limited) series #shiningvale before 12/31. #wearephelps"

Jeff went on to say he was "crushed and deeply saddened" by the decision.

"I am beyond grateful to have been able to tell the saga of the Phelps for the past two seasons," he told Variety, going on to thank the cast and crew.

Fans online shared Jeff's pain.

But all may not be lost. Jeff told Variety that not only is he trying to shop the show to other outlets, he's already working on a season 3.

"I’m hoping another streamer picks the show up as we still have lots of story to tell," he concluded. "And Season 3 has already been partially written.
