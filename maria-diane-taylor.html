Career

Taylor began her musical career at the age of fifteen with Orenda Fink in the band Little Red Rocket. Who Did You Pay (1997) and It’s in the Sound (2000) were the band’s two Geffen releases. The band split up amid the merger of Geffen Records and Universal Music Group. Taylor then relocated to Athens, Georgia, with her musical partner, Fink. They established the band Azure Ray there.

WARM signed the duo, and they published their self-title d debut as well as their second album, Burn and Shiver. Bright Eyes vocalist Conor Oberst brought the band on tour and recommended them to Saddle Creek after meeting them in Athens. Saddle Creek followed up with the November EP and Hold on Love. Taylor and Fink were on a 6-year break after the publication of Hold on Love, during which time they released separate albums. On May 24, 2005, Saddle Creek Records released her first solo album 11:11.

Taylor has also worked with Bright Eyes, Joshua Radin, Moby, Crooked Fingers, Whispertown, Abra Moore, and Nik Freitas. Her music is often included in major TV programs and films. The Azure Ray song “Sleep” was included in the Academy Award-nominated film The Devil Wears Prada, which was released in 2006. Her song “Time Lapse Lifeline” was included in the “Bones” episode “The Beaver In The Otter.” Her music has been used regularly on Grey’s Anatomy–“Song Beneath The Song” from her debut solo album 11:11 appeared on the Grey’s Anatomy Season 1 Soundtrack. Taylor recorded a new song for the show’s 2005 Christmas episode, “Christmas After All,” which she named “Christmas After All.”

She also had a song on the successful ABC Family show Greek. Taylor’s song “No Stars” from her album Lynn Teeter Flower was also included on the prom episode of the CW’s One Tree Hill; she also had a song featured on the show Scrubs. “If Only” (with Conor Oberst) was notably utilized in the final moments of the NBC drama This Is Us’ season one episode nine. Taylor performs with her guitar’s high E-string removed. Maria’s music videos include “Up All Night” from Something About Knowing, directed by Alan Tanner, “Matador” from Overlook, “Song Beneath The Song” from 11:11, “A Good Start” from Lynn Teeter Flower, and “Time Lapse Lifeline” and “Cartoons and Forever Plans” from LadyLuck, directed by SKINNY (Directing Team). Maria also plays drums in the video for “Four Winds” and “New Drinks for the Old Drunk” by Crooked Fingers.
