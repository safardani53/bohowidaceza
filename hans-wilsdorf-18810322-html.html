Entrepreneur Hans Wilsdorf was born on March 22, 1881 in Germany (He dies at the age of 79, on July 6, 1960).

When Wilsdorf learned that personnel of the Royal Air Force who owned Rolex watches were being compelled to hand them over to their captors in POW camps during World War II, he offered to replace the watches for free (until the end of the war, when the former POWs would be expected to pay for their Rolexes).

He was born in Kulmbach, Bavaria, but later moved to Geneva, Switzerland, to run his famous watchmaking company. He co-owned a London-based watch importing company with his wife's brother, Alfred Davis, before moving in Geneva.
