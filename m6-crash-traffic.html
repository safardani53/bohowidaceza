M6 crash involving five cars brings motorway traffic to standstill

A CRASH involving five cars has brought traffic to a standstill on the M6 this morning.

One of the cars is reportedly on fire after the motorway collision in Greater Manchester today.

The crash took place southbound between junction 23, East Lancashire Road, and junction 22, for Newton Le Willows, with delays building up to junction 24 for Ashton In Makerfield.

It is understood the collision involved four cars and a van.

A tweet from Highways England said: "Traffic has been stopped on the M6 Southbound between J23 and J22 due to a vehicle fire & road traffic collision involving 4 cars and 1 van.

"2 lanes now closed Northbound."

North West Motorway Police later tweeted: "Congestion, m6 south bound at junction 23, as a result of a previous collision and car fire.

"It is starting to move passed the scene but it is slow going."

An ambulance spokesperson said: "We received a call at 6.55am. There were five vehicles involved, but there was just one patient reporting chest pains.

“We sent a rapid response vehicle and an ambulance, but we didn’t take anyone to hospital.”
