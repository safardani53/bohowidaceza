Rylan Clark-Neal can't resist giving Holly Willoughby a flirty comment after she flashes a hint of c

RYLAN Clark-Neal made This Morning host Holly Willoughby blush when he told her she looked like a Page 3 model on Wednesday's show.

The 28-year-old made the cheeky remark to the mum-of-three when chatting about her pretty rainbow blouse that revealed a peek of cleavage.

As they sat down to discuss the showbiz news, Rylan said: “You look like a Page 3 model. I’d want to look like that if I were a girl!”

Co-host Phillip Schofield started to laugh as Holly blushed about Rylan's gushing compliment.

The Sun's iconic Page 3 became world famous for launching the careers of some of the UK's biggest models including Melinda Messenger, Sam Fox, Katie Price and Rosie Jones.

The mum-of-three had already turned up the heat on Wednesday morning with one of the outfits chosen by her stylist Angie Smith.

Posing in her dressing room before going live on This Morning, Holly let Angie snap pictures of her wearing a pineapple print dress by Henry Holland.

The cute outfit was cut well above the knee – showing off Holly's tanned, lithe pins.

The £340 dress is high necked with long sleeves that temper the racy hemline.

Angie’s followers fell in love with her styling but some joked the cute outfit was only going to raise eyebrows.

One commented next to the picture: “I love this but I bet the viewers have a heart attack!”

Others simply complimented the styling, with one of Holly’s fans writing: “Love the different looks lately, so refreshing!”

Another wrote: “Looking marvellous Holly.”

Holly, who describes her look as “sophisticated, comfortable and sexy”, sticks to tried-and-tested wardrobe staples and is a devoted advocate of the British high street.

Revealing her fashion rules, she told Glamour UK: “Wear clothes that fit. It’s the most important thing.

“Don’t just see something you like and automatically pick up a size 12 because that’s what you think you are.

“Try on the sizes bigger and smaller than what you expect to be, and buy what genuinely fits and feels most comfortable.”

Holly’s closet is packed full of items from her favourite brands including Karen Millen, LK Bennett, Zara, Office and Marks & Spencer.

And she’s not afraid to mix up high-end designer items such as Roland Mouret with price-friendly garments from H&M.

Holly knows “what works for my body” and now plays it safe with fashion.

She explains: “As I’ve grown up, I definitely have become less experimental; I know what shapes work for me and what doesn’t.

“I don’t tend to follow trends anymore; I like to do my own thing with a nod to what’s trendy at that time.”
